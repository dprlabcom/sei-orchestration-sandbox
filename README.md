---

## 💡 The SEI Methodology

Traditional architectural, manufacturing, and spatial design workflows are heavily bottlenecked by rigid monolithic software, slow manual asset configurations, and non-interoperable rendering engines. 

The SEI pipeline solves this by treating physical reality as **declarative code**. The system completely decouples an asset into two explicit layers of truth:
1. **Master Topology (`_topology.json`):** Dictates absolute geometric primitives, vector tokens, spatial hierarchies, coordinates, and micro-displacement bounds.
2. **Perception Matrix (`_perception_matrix.json`):** Governs physically-based rendering parameters (PBR), thermodynamic material reactions, refractive boundary interfaces (IOR), and complex subsurface scattering maps.

By converting physical space and objects into highly optimized, machine-readable JSON tokens, LLM orchestrators can dynamically read, modify, and render hyper-realistic environments instantly.

## 🍕 Sandbox Assets: Macro-Level Food Realism

To stress-test the pipeline's capabilities without exposing restricted industrial or regional real estate data, this sandbox hosts the complete tokenized schematics for hyper-realistic culinary assets, including our canonical **Artisan Pizza** baseline.

### Technical Stress-Tests Demonstrated:
* **Subsurface Scattering (SSS):** Regulated via the perception matrix to handle shifting light depths within non-uniform organic clusters (e.g., melting mozzarella cheese boundaries).
* **Micro-Displacement Tessellation:** Dictated via topology vector tokens to map porous crust aeration and charred physical topographies.
* **Complex Surface Exudates:** Multi-layered specular and roughness maps tracking lipid/oil sheens on discrete pepperoni geometric instances.

While the demo files in this sandbox focus on organic culinary realism, **this framework is entirely agnostic**. The identical JSON tokenization structure applies seamlessly to modular container systems, consumer manufacturing, kinetic industrial tooling, and complex aerospace geometry.
